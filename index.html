Beaches

Rosses Point

This picturesque village is situated at the entrance to Sligo Harbour from Sligo Bay.

Home to the Sligo Yacht Club, the annual West of Ireland Championship hosted by the County Sligo Golf Club and several Blue Flag strands which are safe for swimming.

Another great event held annually is The Rosses Point Shanty Festival which happens around mid June with its profits going to the RNLI (Royal National Lifeboat Institution). With plenty of places to stay and dine, Rosses Point is bound to be a great destination for trips of any length.

Strandhill

This coastal town in County Sligo is a great place for surfing, but unfortunately it is not safe for swimming.However, there are still plenty of things to do like taking a walk on the beach or going to one of the nearby pubs,restaurants or cafes.

Another great way to spend time here would be to visit The Strandhill People’s Market that takes place at Hangar 1 at Sligo Airport. The Market happens every Sunday from 11am to 4pm and offers an array of local crafts, foods, textiles and live music.

Town attractions

Sligo Abbey

This Dominican convent founded in 1253 was built in the Romanesque style. The beautiful extensive ruins of mainly the church and the cloister ramain for tourists to visit.

abbey contains a well-preserved cloister, a great wealth of carvings (as well as Gothic and Renaissance tomb sculpture) and the only sculptured fifteenth-century high altar to survive in an Irish monastic church.

Yeats Building

This beautiful 19th century red brick building is home of the Yeats Society Sligo.

It is the headquarters of the Yeats International Summer Festivals and houses the Yeats Society Library, the Sligo Art Gallery, and a photographic collection of Yeats and his family. This is a great place to visit and find out more about Yeats’ life and his work.

Nearby attractions

Lisadell House and Gardens

This majestic place is situated on the shores of Sligo Bay.

Surrounded by the natural beauty of the Atlantic Ocean, woodland and mountains, Lissadell House and Gardens has also historic and literary associations, as it was the childhood home of Constance Markievicz, her sister Eva Gore-Booth and her brother Josslyn Gore-Booth.

Mullaghmore

This holiday destination is located one the Mullaghmore peninsula in County Sligo, Ireland.

Its skyline is dominated by Ben Bulben mountain and the Atlantic Ocean.

Here, you can stroll along the beautiful white sandy beach or go into the water for a swim or surfing session.

This is Ireland’s ultimate big wave destination so no matter the reason for your trip the incredible views are always guaranteed.
